What is Lyme Disease?


Lyme disease is the common term for the disease transmitted by Borrelia burgdorferi, a bacterium whose common vector in North America is the blacklegged or deer tick, Ixodes scapularis. Lyme disease can affect a variety of species, includes deer, mice, horses, dogs, and humans.
